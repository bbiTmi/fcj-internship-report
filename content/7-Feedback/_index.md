### General Feedback

**1. Working Environment**  
The working environment is very friendly and open. Members of FCJ are always willing to help whenever I encounter difficulties, even outside working hours. The workspace is tidy and comfortable, helping me stay focused and productive.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admin**  
The guidance and teamwork throughout the program were excellent. The team was always ready to discuss technical challenges, review my suggestions, and guide me through complex AWS concepts. What I appreciated most was that the mentor encouraged me to research and propose solutions on my own before offering support.

**3. Alignment Between Tasks and My Academic Major**  
Participating in FCJ allowed me to learn additional skills and new Cloud services, which helped expand my understanding of how different industries operate. These are valuable soft skills that will benefit my future career.

**4. Learning Opportunities & Skill Development**  
During the internship, I gained various new skills such as using project management tools, collaborating effectively in a team, and communicating professionally in a corporate environment. My mentor also shared many real-world insights that helped me better shape my career direction.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The company culture is extremely positive: everyone respects each other, works seriously yet maintains a cheerful atmosphere.

### Additional Questions

- What are you **most satisfied with** during your internship?  
The flexible and comfortable working time, positive environment, and friendly team members who always supported me enthusiastically whenever I needed help.

- What do you think the company should **improve** for future interns?  
More internal events so that members have more opportunities to connect with each other.

- Would you **recommend this internship** to your friends? Why?  
**Absolutely yes.** FCJ offers a dynamic and friendly environment, along with great opportunities for personal and professional development.
